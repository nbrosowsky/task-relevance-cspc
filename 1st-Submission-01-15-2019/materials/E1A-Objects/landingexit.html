Experimental Debriefing

Thank you for participating in our experiment!

This document serves as short description of the purposes behind our experiment.

The general purpose of this study is to investigate spatial aspects of human attention.

You just completed what is commonly known as a Flanker task. On each trial you were presented with five arrows pointed left or right. Sometimes the center arrow was pointed in the same direction as the other four, and sometimes it was pointed in the opposite direction. Typically people are slower to respond when the center arrow is pointed in the opposite direction. This is thought to reflect your ability to ignore conflicting information (the slower you are, the harder it is to ignore the other arrows).

The current experiment investigated whether people can learn about image features associated with more or less conflicting information (e.g., the color blue, the hat image, etc.). For example, if you learn that blue items are generally associated with conflict, you might be able to use that as a cue for more focused attention. The counting task was used to get you to focus on certain features more than others.
